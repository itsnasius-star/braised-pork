# Research Log

## Project Topic

YouTube content strategy for B2B SaaS.

## Reasoning

I chose this topic because it balances research quality, technical feasibility, and practical usefulness. YouTube content is public, transcript-friendly, and easier to collect systematically without relying on paid tools or restricted platform scraping.

The topic is also relevant to B2B SaaS because many SaaS founders, marketers, and operators use video, podcasts, interviews, webinars, and educational content to build trust, explain complex products, generate demand, and repurpose content across other channels.

## Research Approach

This project prioritizes high-signal sources over volume. I will focus on practitioners who actively create, operate, or teach B2B SaaS growth, content, positioning, demand generation, or founder-led marketing.

The collected material should be strong enough to support a practical B2B SaaS YouTube/content strategy playbook later.

## Selection Criteria

I selected experts and channels using these criteria:

1. They are relevant to B2B SaaS, SaaS growth, positioning, demand generation, or content strategy.
2. They publish educational or interview-based content that can be analyzed through transcripts.
3. They are practitioners, founders, operators, or recognized specialists, not only generic commentators.
4. Their content includes repeatable lessons, frameworks, examples, or tactical insights.
5. Their material is public and feasible to collect using free tools or manual fallback methods.
